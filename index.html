Why Traditional Lawns Waste Water, Money, and Time

Most Colorado homeowners inherited a traditional lawn without questioning it. It’s green, it’s familiar, and it’s supposed to add curb appeal. But in a state where water is scarce and summers are unpredictable, that lawn is quietly working against you.

The average Colorado household spends $500–$1,200 per year on lawn watering alone — before you factor in fertilizer, pest treatments, and the hours spent mowing every week from May through October.

Colorado averages just 14 inches of rain per year. Traditional turf needs three times that to stay green. Every time you run the sprinklers, you’re fighting the climate you live in.

Draining Your Wallet

Water, fertilizer, mowing, and seasonal treatments cost the average Colorado homeowner over $1,000 a year — for a lawn you barely use.

Eating Your Weekends
